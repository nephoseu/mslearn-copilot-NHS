# Explore Microsoft Copilot in Microsoft Edge (NHS Scenario)

Welcome to the **Microsoft Copilot in Microsoft Edge** lab, adapted for an **NHS service improvement scenario**.

In this exercise, you will use **Copilot in Microsoft Edge (web experience)** to explore, research, and refine a **non-clinical NHS service improvement idea** for a fictional organisation: **Northbridge NHS Foundation Trust**.

This lab aligns with the same **scenario and flow** used in the Microsoft 365 Copilot version of the exercise, but is designed for environments **without a Microsoft 365 Copilot license**, using Copilot in Edge instead.


> **Important**  
> This exercise provides example prompts you can use with Copilot. Use them as a *starting point*. You are encouraged to modify prompts and iterate.
> Some of the content in this course was generated with AI. Generative AI is non-deterministic and results may vary and are unpredictable.

This exercise should take approximately **40 minutes** to complete.

> **Note**  
> This exercise requires a **Microsoft 365 Copilot** license.

> **Safety and Governance Notice**  
> - Fictional training scenario  
> - No patient-identifiable information (PII)  
> - No clinical decision-making  
> - Human review required for all outputs
> - Human review required for all outputs

> **NHS specific information**
>
> You can consult the Microsoft 365 Copilot acceptable use policy at this link: https://support.nhs.net/knowledge-base/m365-copilot-acceptable-use-policy/
>
> For information about the using information protection and sensitivity labels, please consult: https://support.nhs.net/knowledge-base/sensitivity-labels-overview/

> **Note**  
> This exercise assumes you are signed into **Microsoft Edge with a organizational account for your Microsoft 365 services**

## Use Copilot in Edge to explore a service improvement idea

To begin, you will use **Microsoft Copilot in Edge** to explore and refine an NHS service improvement idea.

### Step 1: Review the business idea document

1. In Microsoft Edge:

   Download **Business Idea – NHS Copilot Pilot.docx** from the training repository [Business Idea – NHS Copilot Pilot.docx](https://github.com/nephoseu/mslearn-copilot-NHS/raw/main/Allfiles/Business%20Idea%20%E2%80%93%20NHS%20Copilot%20Pilot.docx).

2. When the document opens, choose **Edit a copy** to save it to your OneDrive.

3. Review the document, which outlines a draft **NHS service improvement proposal** focused on reducing administrative burden and improving operational flow.

---

### Step 2: Use Copilot in Edge to summarise the idea

1. With the document open in Edge, open **Copilot**.
2. Enter the following prompt:

```prompt
Summarise the key problem this NHS Trust is trying to address and the proposed approach.
```

3. Review the response.

4. Ask a follow-up question:

```prompt
What are the key operational risks and constraints in this proposal?
```

5. Research pilot setup steps:

```prompt
What are the typical steps to start a productivity pilot using Microsoft 365 Copilot in an NHS Trust?
Answer with a numbered list.
```

---

## Research NHS context using Copilot in Edge

You will now use Copilot in Edge to research **publicly available NHS context** relevant to the proposal.

### Step 3: Research outpatient and administrative challenges

In Copilot, enter:

```prompt
What are common administrative and operational challenges faced by NHS Trusts in England?
```

Review the response and sources provided.

Follow up with:

```prompt
What non-clinical approaches are commonly used to reduce administrative burden in NHS organisations?
```

---

### Step 4: Research how NHS productivity pilots are typically run

Ask Copilot:

```prompt
How do NHS Trusts typically run small productivity or digital pilots?
Provide a high-level list of steps.
```

When satisfied, copy the list and paste it into the **Business Idea.docx** document.

Use Word (in Edge) to convert the list into a **table**, adding columns for:
- Owner
- Governance considerations

---

## Refine the service improvement idea

You will now use Copilot in Edge to improve the proposal content.

### Step 5: Improve feasibility and clarity

Ask Copilot:

```prompt
Suggest improvements to this NHS service improvement idea, focusing on feasibility, governance, and operational impact.
```

Optionally refine further:

```prompt
Rewrite this idea for an NHS executive audience. Keep it concise and focused on assurance.
```

Update the **Business Idea.docx** document with the improved wording.

---

## Prepare leadership-ready outputs

Although Copilot in Edge cannot directly generate Microsoft 365 content, you can still use it to **draft text and structure** for downstream use.

### Step 6: Draft a board briefing outline

Ask Copilot:

```prompt
Create an outline for a board-level briefing to sponsor an NHS productivity pilot.
Include purpose, scope, risks, governance, and success measures.
```

Copy the outline into your document.

---

### Step 7: Draft an executive email

Ask Copilot:

```prompt
Write a concise email to an NHS Director of Operations requesting sponsorship for a 12-week productivity pilot.
Use a professional NHS tone and include governance assurances.
```

Review and refine the draft.

---

### Step 8: Creating an document image

Ask Copilot:

```prompt
Generate me a picture that I would include as leading picture in the business idea document.
```

Review and refine the draft.

---

### Step 9: Use Copilot to generate and visualize financial projections

Ask Copilot:

```prompt
Create a simple 5-year benefit model for an NHS productivity pilot using reasonable assumptions.
```

Review the response, which should include a table of simple benefit model for the next five years.

If an option to edit the table in Excel is shown, select it to create a new workbook with the table of data. Otherwise, return to the OneDrive tab and, use the **(+)** button to add a new **Excel workbook**. Then copy the table of data from the Copilot pane into the workbook.

Change the name of the workbook to `Benefit model`.

In the Copilot pane, enter the following prompt:

    ```prompt
    What's a good way to visualize these projections in a chart?
    ```

Review the response, which should recommend a few ways to visualize the projections data. Then enter the following prompt:

    ```prompt
    How do I create a line chart in Excel?
    ```

Follow the guidance provided by Copilot to create a line chart.

### Step 10: Use Copilot to create content for a presentation

Now you'll need an effective presentation to convince your board

In OneDrive, add a new **PowerPoint presentation**. If the **Designer** pane opens automatically, close it. Then change the name of the presentation from *Presentation* to *NHS Copilot Pilot Briefing.pptx*.

On the title slide for the presentation, enter the the name of your fund as the title, and `Business improvement proposal` as the subtitle.

Insert a new slide, using the **Two Content** slide layout (which includes a title and two placeholders for content).

Change the slide title to `12-week pilot - "Outpatient Flow Accelerator"`.

In the Copilot pane, enter following prompt:

```prompt
Summarize project in the attached file (reference your word document from the OneDrive) into 5 bullet points.
Include challenge, scope, governance, benefits and risks.
```
Copy Copilot's response to the clipboard, and paste it into the left content placeholder. Then edit and reformat the text in the placeholder until you are satisfied.

In the Copilot pane, enter the following prompt:
```prompt
Create a photorealistic image of smiling and happy doctor and patient.
```
When Copilot has generated an image you like, copy it to the clipboard and paste it into the content placeholder on the right of the slide.

If the **Designer** pane opens automatically, select a slide design you like. Then close the **Designer** pane.

Apply any additional reformatting you think is required until you have a slide that you're happy with.

### Step 11: Use Copilot to create content for a presentation

You've created some collateral to help you get your business started. Now it's time to reach out to a Director of Operations.

1. Use the **App Launcher** (&#8759;) at the left end of the OneDrive title bar to open **Outlook**.

1. On the **Mail** page, create a new email. Fill in the **To** box with your own email address and set the **Subject** to `Sponsorship request for the Outpatient Flow Accelerator pilot`.
1. In the Copilot pane, enter the following prompt:

    ```prompt
    Write a concise email to the Director of Operations requesting sponsorship for the Outpatient Flow Accelerator pilot.
    Use a professional NHS tone.
    ```
