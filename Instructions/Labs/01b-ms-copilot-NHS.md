# Explore Microsoft Copilot in Microsoft Edge (NHS Scenario)

Welcome to the **Microsoft Copilot in Microsoft Edge** lab, adapted for an **NHS service improvement scenario**.

In this exercise, you will use **Copilot in Microsoft Edge (web experience)** to explore, research, and refine a **non-clinical NHS service improvement idea** for a fictional organisation: **Northbridge NHS Foundation Trust**.

This lab aligns with the same **scenario and flow** used in the Microsoft 365 Copilot version of the exercise, but is designed for environments **without a Microsoft 365 Copilot license**, using Copilot in Edge instead.

> **Important**  
> - This is a fictional training scenario  
> - No patient-identifiable information (PII) is used  
> - Copilot is not used for clinical decision-making  
> - All outputs must be reviewed by a human and comply with NHS Information Governance (IG) policies

This exercise should take approximately **35–40 minutes** to complete.

> **Note**  
> This exercise assumes you are signed into **Microsoft Edge with a personal Microsoft account** (for example, outlook.com).

---

## Use Copilot in Edge to explore a service improvement idea

To begin, you will use **Microsoft Copilot in Edge** to explore and refine an NHS service improvement idea.

### Step 1: Review the business idea document

1. In Microsoft Edge, open the following document:

   **Business Idea.docx** (NHS version provided in the training repository)

2. When the document opens, choose **Edit a copy** to save it to your OneDrive.

3. Review the document, which outlines a draft **NHS service improvement proposal** focused on reducing administrative burden and improving operational flow.

---

### Step 2: Use Copilot in Edge to summarise the idea

1. With the document open in Edge, open **Copilot**.
2. Enter the following prompt:

```prompt
Summarise the key problem this NHS Trust is trying to address and the proposed approach.
```

3. Review the response.

4. Ask a follow-up question:

```prompt
What are the main risks, assumptions, and constraints in this proposal?
```

---

## Research NHS context using Copilot in Edge

You will now use Copilot in Edge to research **publicly available NHS context** relevant to the proposal.

### Step 3: Research outpatient and administrative challenges

In Copilot, enter:

```prompt
What are common administrative and operational challenges faced by NHS Trusts in England?
```

Review the response and sources provided.

Follow up with:

```prompt
What non-clinical approaches are commonly used to reduce administrative burden in NHS organisations?
```

---

### Step 4: Research how NHS productivity pilots are typically run

Ask Copilot:

```prompt
How do NHS Trusts typically run small productivity or digital pilots?
Provide a high-level list of steps.
```

When satisfied, copy the list and paste it into the **Business Idea.docx** document.

Use Word (in Edge) to convert the list into a **table**, adding columns for:
- Owner
- Governance considerations

---

## Refine the service improvement idea

You will now use Copilot in Edge to improve the proposal content.

### Step 5: Improve feasibility and clarity

Ask Copilot:

```prompt
Suggest improvements to this NHS service improvement idea, focusing on feasibility, governance, and operational impact.
```

Optionally refine further:

```prompt
Rewrite this idea for an NHS executive audience. Keep it concise and focused on assurance.
```

Update the **Business Idea.docx** document with the improved wording.

---

## Prepare leadership-ready outputs

Although Copilot in Edge cannot directly generate Microsoft 365 content, you can still use it to **draft text and structure** for downstream use.

### Step 6: Draft a board briefing outline

Ask Copilot:

```prompt
Create an outline for a board-level briefing to sponsor an NHS productivity pilot.
Include purpose, scope, risks, governance, and success measures.
```

Copy the outline into your document.

---

### Step 7: Draft an executive email

Ask Copilot:

```prompt
Write a concise email to an NHS Director of Operations requesting sponsorship for a 12-week productivity pilot.
Use a professional NHS tone and include governance assurances.
```

Review and refine the draft.

## Conclusion

In this exercise, you used **Microsoft Copilot in Edge** to:
- Explore and refine an NHS service improvement idea

This demonstrates how Copilot can support **early-stage thinking and research** in NHS organisations, even without a Microsoft 365 Copilot license.
